About me

I'm here because I believe everyone deserves a safe place to be themselves.

"The curious paradox is that when I accept myself just as I am, then I can change." — Carl Rogers

I'm currently completing my practicum as a student counsellor. I work from a person-centred foundation — which means I follow your lead. You are the expert on your own life, and my role is to listen deeply, reflect honestly, and walk alongside you without judgment.

I weave in attachment theory to help us understand the patterns that shape how you relate to yourself and others, and draw on an integrative lens to tailor our work to what resonates with you.

Person-Centred Attachment-Informed Integrative Non-Judgmental Trauma-Aware

How I work

What you can expect from our sessions

Every person is different, and so is every session. Here are the threads that run through all the work I do.

Unconditional positive regard

You will not be judged, rushed, or told what to feel. I meet you exactly where you are — with full acceptance and genuine care.

Exploring your patterns

Together we can gently look at the relational patterns you carry — where they came from and whether they still serve you today.

A safe, consistent space

Confidentiality and consistency matter. Each session is yours — a predictable, private container where you can say what you actually mean.

Your pace, always

There's no agenda to rush through. We go at the speed that feels right for you — and you are always free to steer the conversation.

Tailored to you

My integrative approach means I draw from different frameworks based on what seems most helpful for you — not a one-size-fits-all script.

Growth, gently

Insight alone isn't always enough. We work toward meaningful, sustainable change — one that grows from understanding, not pressure.

Group program

Therapeutic D&D: grow through the game

Dungeons & Dragons isn't just a game — it's a surprisingly powerful space for building real-world skills. In this therapeutic group, we use collaborative storytelling and roleplay to explore identity, practice communication, and develop social and emotional skills in a low-pressure, imaginative setting.

No experience with D&D is needed — just a willingness to show up, try things, and be part of a supportive group. I'll guide the adventure.

Small group format Run online with a small group of participants for an intimate, safe experience.
Weekly sessions Regular weekly meetups — timing to be confirmed based on group availability.
